Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1 Sun Aug 10 / Mon Aug 11

Arrival in Geneva → Lausanne

Flight & Transport

  • Flight LX 23: JFK 7:25 PM → Geneva 9:15 AM (Tue Aug 11)
  • Train to Lausanne: 1 hr direct from Geneva Airport
  • Check-in: Royal Savoy Hotel & Spa (~11:00 AM)

Activities

  • Recover from overnight flight and jet lag
  • Explore Lausanne lakefront (stroller-friendly)
  • Early dinner and bedtime for toddler

Keep Day 1 light to adjust to time zone.

Day 2 Mon Aug 11

Montreux & Lake Geneva

Morning

  • Sleep in and adjust to Swiss time
  • Train to Montreux (20 min from Lausanne)

Montreux

  • Lakeside Promenade: stroller walk along Lake Geneva
  • Château de Chillon: lakeside castle (exterior viewing)
  • Charlie Chaplin statue and waterfront
  • Lunch with lake views

Evening

  • Return to Lausanne for afternoon nap
  • Optional: $100 experience credit at Royal Savoy spa

Alternative: Golden Pass Railway (Montreux → Gstaad)

Day 3 Tue Aug 12

Lausanne → Luzern

Morning

  • Check out of Royal Savoy
  • Golden Pass Railway to Luzern (2.5 hrs scenic) or regular train

Afternoon in Luzern

  • Check into hotel
  • Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke): 14th-century wooden bridge
  • Old Town: colorful buildings, cobblestone streets
  • Walk along Lake Luzern waterfront

"The gateway to Central Switzerland" — medieval charm meets mountain scenery.

Day 4 Wed Aug 13

Mt. Rigi — Queen of the Mountains

Boat + cogwheel train + 360° views — perfect for toddlers! Included with Swiss Travel Pass.

Morning Route

  • Lake cruise: Luzern → Vitznau
  • Cogwheel railway: Vitznau → Rigi Kulm
  • Summit: 360° views, easy walks, mountain restaurant

Afternoon Return

  • Descent: Rigi Staffel → Rigi Kaltbad
  • Cable car: Rigi Kaltbad → Weggis
  • Boat: Weggis → Luzern
Day 5 Thu Aug 14

Luzern → Grindelwald

Morning

  • Optional: Swiss Transport Museum (great for toddlers)
  • Check out of Luzern hotel

Travel

  • Train: Luzern → Interlaken → Grindelwald (2 hrs)
  • Arrive at base of the Eiger

Afternoon

  • Check in (4 nights)
  • Explore Alpine village, car-free zones
  • Settle in and unpack

Grindelwald sits at the foot of the iconic Eiger North Face.

Day 6 Fri Aug 15

Grindelwald First & Cliff Walk

Bucket list mountain experience — don't forget your camera!

Morning — First Cliff Walk

  • Cable car: Grindelwald → First (25 min, toddler in carrier)
  • First Cliff Walk: suspension walkway with dramatic views
  • Mountain restaurant with panoramic terrace
  • Optional: First Flyer zipline, Mountain Cart (adults take turns)

Afternoon

  • Return for toddler nap
  • Village exploration, playground
Day 7 Sat Aug 16

Lauterbrunnen — Valley of 72 Waterfalls

Vertical rock faces rising 300m on both sides — one of the most beautiful valleys in the Alps.

Morning — Staubbach Falls

  • Train: Grindelwald → Lauterbrunnen (15 min)
  • Staubbach Falls: 300m waterfall, walk right up (stroller-friendly)
  • Valley floor walk: flat path, dramatic cliffs

Trümmelbach Falls

  • 10 glacier-fed waterfalls inside the mountain
  • Accessible by elevator and short walks

Afternoon

  • Option A: Cable car to Mürren (car-free village)
  • Option B: Return to Grindelwald for nap
Day 8 Sun Aug 17

Jungfraujoch — Top of Europe

Europe's highest railway station at 11,332 ft. WEATHER DEPENDENT — clear days only!

Morning Journey

  • Early start: 7:30–8:00 AM
  • Train: Grindelwald → Kleine Scheidegg → Jungfraujoch
  • ~1 hr each way, scenic mountain railway

At Summit

  • Sphinx Observatory: highest viewing platform in Europe
  • Ice Palace: glacier ice tunnels with sculptures
  • Aletsch Glacier views

Toddler Tips

  • DRESS VERY WARMLY — freezing at top even in August
  • Watch for altitude fussiness
  • May nap on train
Day 9 Mon Aug 18

Flexible Day — Choose by Weather & Energy

A. Zermatt & Matterhorn

2.5 hrs each way — ambitious. Gornergrat Railway. Only if toddler handling travel well.

B. Bachalpsee Lake Hike

Cable car to First, 1-hr hike (toddler in carrier). Mirror-like alpine lake.

C. Interlaken

20 min train. Lake Thun or Brienz boat cruise. Easier, relaxed.

D. Rest Day

Village walks, playground, laundry, pack for departure.

Don't push too hard on the last full day.

Essential Planning

Swiss Travel Pass

  • 8-Day Pass for 4 adults: ~$2,080
  • Toddler: FREE with Family Card
  • Unlimited trains, buses, boats, 500+ museums
  • 50% off Jungfraujoch, free Mt. Rigi

Jungfraujoch tickets ~$400 · Total transport ~$2,480

Packing for Toddler

  • Clothing: Layers, rain gear, warm jacket (mountains!), walking shoes
  • Gear: Lightweight stroller, baby carrier, portable high chair
  • Jungfraujoch: Winter coat, hat, gloves
  • Supplies: Diapers, wipes, snacks, sippy cups
  • Entertainment: Small toys, books, tablet with downloads

Toddler Travel Tips

  • Build in 2–3 hr afternoon naps daily
  • Train rides = perfect nap time; bring entertainment for wake times
  • Have backup plans for meltdowns
  • Early starts (7–8 AM) before crowds
  • Always have emergency snacks and water
  • Early dinners (6–7 PM) for toddler bedtime

This itinerary balances iconic Swiss experiences with realistic pacing for a family with a toddler. The 3-base strategy provides variety while minimizing hotel changes. Switzerland's excellent train system makes this trip manageable even with young children.

Most important: Don't try to do everything! Switzerland isn't going anywhere. Better to enjoy what you do see rather than rush through attractions. The journey itself — trains through mountains, boat rides on lakes — is the experience.
